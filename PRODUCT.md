## Register
product

## Users
Chinese high-school graduates, incoming college students, and students comparing universities, dorms, majors, admissions guidance, and campus-learning resources on mobile and desktop.

## Purpose
Help students quickly choose and compare schools, read practical admission/freshman/self-improvement articles, and understand campus life data without getting lost in messy source documents.

## Brand Personality
Practical, trustworthy, calm, student-friendly, information-dense but readable.

## Anti-References
Garbled imported documents, walls of undifferentiated text, duplicated PDF tables of contents, tiny dense exam-question blobs, modal layouts that trap or crowd content, ornamental UI that slows task completion.

## Strategic Design Principles
1. Preserve source value but never expose source clutter: strip field codes, escaped unicode, duplicated TOCs, malformed URLs, and parsing artifacts before rendering.
2. Reading comes first: prose must have clear paragraphs, headings, lists, question/answer boundaries, and comfortable line length.
3. Dense study materials need structure: questions, options, answers, pinyin, and formulas must be visually separable at a glance.
4. Fast perceived performance: cache stable content, avoid re-fetching unchanged data, render large articles progressively, and keep interactions responsive.
5. Use a consistent product vocabulary: restrained colors, standard controls, accessible focus/hover states, and mobile-safe modals.

## Accessibility
Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-accessible dialogs and navigation, readable Chinese typography with 65–75ch prose line length, and responsive layouts for phone-first students.
